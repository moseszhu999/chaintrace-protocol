# ChainTrace Manifesto

Regulation cannot reach every corner of global trade.

Full transparency is neither possible nor desirable, because commerce needs privacy, negotiation space, and business secrets.

But without verifiable trust, honest small businesses are punished, fraud spreads, and bad actors drive good actors out of the market.

ChainTrace exists to fill this gap: not by exposing everything, but by making critical facts verifiable.

## Core Belief

**Proof, not exposure.**

Commercial privacy matters. A business should not be forced to reveal every contract term, every customer relationship, every supplier, or every pricing detail in order to be trusted.

But some facts need to be verifiable:

- a file existed at a certain time
- a shipment event was submitted by a specific actor
- a batch was inspected
- a buyer accepted delivery
- an invoice is linked to real trade evidence
- an attestation was signed by a known party

ChainTrace focuses on these critical facts.

## Why It Matters

Most small businesses do not want to cheat. They want to sell, deliver, get paid, and build long-term relationships.

The problem is that honest businesses often lack a low-cost way to prove their honesty. A few bad actors can raise the trust cost for everyone.

This is how bad actors drive good actors out of the market.

ChainTrace aims to reverse that cycle.

## Design Principles

### 1. Boundaries, not control

ChainTrace does not design every supply chain workflow.

It defines a minimal boundary for verifiable facts:

- identity or address
- evidence hash
- URI or CID
- timestamp
- signature
- attestation
- verification path

Within that boundary, users and markets can create their own order.

### 2. Open protocol, emergent order

Existing ERP, logistics, trade, quality inspection, finance, and SaaS platforms do not need to be replaced.

They only need a simple way to anchor critical facts on-chain.

### 3. Small businesses first

The long-term goal is infrastructure.

The starting point is simple: help a small business create a proof page for a product, shipment, invoice, or trade record.

### 4. AI agents need verifiable facts

AI agents can read, summarize, compare, and coordinate information.

But they also need a trusted fact layer.

ChainTrace gives physical supply chains a verifiable memory and gives AI agents a coordination layer based on signed facts.

## Mission Statement

Build a verifiable trust layer for honest small businesses.

Make honesty cheaper.  
Make fraud more expensive.  
Enable open order from verifiable facts.
