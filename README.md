# ChainTrace Protocol

**A verifiable fact layer for global supply chains, small businesses, and AI agents.**

ChainTrace is an open protocol for anchoring supply chain facts, evidence hashes, signatures, timestamps, and attestations on-chain.

It helps small businesses prove their products, shipments, invoices, and trade history without exposing sensitive business data.

## Mission

Global trade does not suffer from a lack of goods.  
It suffers from a lack of low-cost verifiable trust.

Many honest small businesses cannot easily prove their products, shipments, invoices, or trade history. Meanwhile, a small number of bad actors increase the trust cost for everyone.

ChainTrace exists to make honesty cheaper and fraud more expensive.

**Not total transparency. Selective proof.**

## What ChainTrace Does

ChainTrace allows users, businesses, platforms, and AI agents to create verifiable records for:

- product proofs
- shipment proofs
- invoice proofs
- trade history
- supply chain events
- attestations
- receivable proof packs

## Core Objects

- **Proof** — evidence hash, URI, timestamp, and submitter
- **Batch** — product batch, shipment, order, or trade object
- **Event** — supply chain event linked to a batch
- **Artifact** — file, image, invoice, report, or document
- **Attestation** — signed statement about a fact
- **Business Passport** — verifiable business history

## Philosophy

ChainTrace is not another supply chain SaaS.

It does not replace ERP, logistics platforms, trade platforms, or finance systems.

Instead, ChainTrace provides an open fact layer where existing systems, small businesses, and AI agents can anchor critical facts.

We set the boundary.  
The market grows the order.

## Early Product Direction

The first user-facing product will be a simple proof page:

1. Upload evidence.
2. Generate a file hash.
3. Anchor the hash on-chain.
4. Share a public verification page or QR code.

Over time, these proofs can grow into a **Business Passport**: a public, verifiable trade history for small businesses.
